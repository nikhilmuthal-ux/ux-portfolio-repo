01 · Objective

The problem

Teams get far too many alerts. They arrive faster than anyone can read them, the useful details are spread across many tools, and nothing explains why an alert matters. When everything looks urgent, the alerts that really matter get missed.

The goal

Build an AI helper that feels calm, not noisy. It should spot small problems early, show only what matters, and always explain how it got there, so people can act with confidence instead of guessing.

She should notice the little things before they become big problems.

07 · Conversation

History, search, and answers you can trust.

TESSA is more than one question box. Chats are saved and searchable, you can switch models right in the chat, and you can check or act on any answer.

Saved

Every chat is saved and named, so you can come back to it later.

10 · Reflection

The hardest part wasn't making TESSA smart. It was making her feel quiet.

Holding back was the real design work. Every time we wanted to add another number, alert, or animation, we asked if it was worth the user's attention. Usually it was not, and leaving it out made the important moments stand out more.

If I did it again, I would focus on the "why" sooner. Showing how TESSA reaches an answer built the most trust, and it should have shaped the design from day one.
