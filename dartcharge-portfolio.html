The challenge

When a payment system fails, users pay twice

Since 2014 the Dartford Crossing has had no toll booths and no barriers. Cameras read number plates at full speed. Drivers must know, entirely on their own, that they have to pay later, a rule the road never stops to explain.

Most fines happened because of confusion, not cheating. People wanted to pay, the system made it hard.

The approach

Four principles shaped every decision

01

Task first, account later

The most urgent job is “I just crossed, let me pay.” Sign-up comes after payment, never before it.

02

Deadlines are design elements

The most important information gets real design attention: one element, exact dates, shown everywhere, not a line of text.

03

No silent states

The system must never hide anything about your account. Every problem is visible, flagged early, and easy to fix.

04

Self-service replaces phone calls

Every common support question gets a screen that answers it. Fewer phone calls was the business case.
